Chapter 1: Arrival

April pressed her nose to the glass window of the train as the sprawling skyline of Curio City appeared beyond the mist. Spires twisted like vines into the clouds, and buildings shimmered with strange lights that danced like fireflies.

"This place looks like a dream," she whispered.

John leaned over, squinting. “More like a puzzle someone built with spare parts.”

They’d arrived on a school field trip—though this was no ordinary city, and no ordinary school outing. Curio City was only open to visitors for seven days every hundred years. And today marked the first day of its return.

The moment they stepped off the train, the air felt charged. A streetlamp winked at them. A newspaper stand shifted slightly, as if it had feet. The cobblestones beneath their shoes gave a soft chuckle.

April grinned. “Definitely a dream.”

“Or a trap,” John muttered. But he followed her anyway.


Chapter 2: The Map of Oddities

At the city's edge, an old woman in a velvet cloak handed them a parchment.

“Every visitor gets one,” she croaked. “A map of the odd, the curious, and the just plain impossible.”

April unrolled it. Instead of streets and squares, the map changed as she looked—sometimes showing a library inside a tree trunk, other times a staircase floating midair.

John poked the paper. “I don’t trust anything that shifts when you’re not watching it.”

“Then don’t blink,” April said with a smirk.

They decided to follow the trail to something called The Whispering Gallery. As they walked, they passed lampposts that played music, statues that sneezed, and vending machines that offered bottled laughter.

“April,” John said slowly, “what if this place isn’t just curious… what if it’s alive?”

April looked around. The city did feel like it was watching. Waiting.

“Then let’s hope it likes us,” she said.


Chapter 3: Secrets and Choices

The Whispering Gallery was a circular hall made of mirrors. Inside, their reflections didn’t copy their movements—they moved on their own. One version of April stuck out her tongue. Another John waved solemnly.

Suddenly, a whisper echoed through the room:

"Every curious soul must choose: return to the world you know… or stay and uncover the secrets of the city."

April blinked. “Stay forever?”

John’s eyes widened. “I don’t know if it’s a threat or an invitation.”

The mirrors pulsed with light. A doorway appeared in one of them—showing their train waiting to leave. Another mirror showed a towering library with glowing books and a city that went deeper underground than up.

April looked at John. “I want to know more.”

John hesitated… then nodded. “Let’s stay. For now.”

As they stepped through the mirror into the heart of the city, the map in April’s hand shimmered and settled. It had found their path.

The City of Curiosity had accepted them.

And their adventure was just beginning.
