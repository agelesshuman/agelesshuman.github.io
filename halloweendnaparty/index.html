Halloween DNA Party

Chapter 1: The Invitation

It all began with a strange invitation that appeared on every doorstep in the quiet town of Genomica. Folded in deep purple envelopes sealed with a silver double helix, the message was short and cryptic:

“You are cordially invited to the Halloween DNA Party. One night only. Midnight. The Old Biolabs. Come as your true self.”

Most people thought it was a joke—just another themed Halloween bash. But those who knew the old Biolabs, now a long-abandoned research facility, raised an eyebrow. It had been closed for decades after a series of mysterious experiments. Rumors spoke of genetic experiments gone wrong, chimeras roaming the night, and a missing scientist known only as Dr. Gene.

Despite the warnings, curiosity got the better of many. Among them were three teens: Maya, a science nerd; Zack, a prankster with a love for horror; and Lex, a quiet coder who always wore gloves—even in summer.

“I bet it’s a high-tech haunted house,” Zack said as they walked up the ivy-covered steps of the Biolabs that Halloween night. Maya, clutching her DNA-model costume, wasn’t so sure.

The heavy doors creaked open by themselves. Fog rolled out like it had been waiting. Inside was a glowing dance floor shaped like a DNA double helix. Strange figures danced under blacklights. Some had tails. Others had feathers. A few were...hard to describe.

Lex hesitated. “This feels real,” they whispered.

A deep voice boomed from the upper balcony. “Welcome to the Halloween DNA Party! Tonight, unleash your true genetic potential! Let your DNA decide your costume!”

Then everything went black.


Chapter 2: Genetic Revelations

When the lights flickered back on, something had changed.

Maya gasped. Her fingers glowed with bioluminescence. Zack had patches of scaly skin, and his eyes blinked sideways like a reptile. Lex had sprouted faint, iridescent wings.

“What—what’s happening?!” Maya stammered.

A floating screen appeared in front of them. It scanned each of them quickly and displayed a hologram:

  • Maya: Carrier of bioluminescent cephalopod gene – ACTIVATED
  • Zack: Dormant reptilian adaptation gene – ACTIVATED
  • Lex: Unknown hybrid gene signature – ACTIVATED

A figure in a lab coat stepped into view—face hidden behind a glowing mask shaped like a skull. “Your DNA holds secrets passed down from ancestors, evolution, and...enhancement,” the voice echoed. “This is no costume party. This is your awakening.”

Maya, always curious, stepped forward. “Are you Dr. Gene?”

The figure nodded. “Yes. And I created this party to unlock the next stage of humanity—through celebration, through fear, through truth.”

Zack laughed nervously. “So… I’m part lizard now?”

“Only tonight—unless you choose otherwise.”

Other guests were starting to notice their own transformations. Some panicked. Others danced harder, celebrating their new forms. One girl turned invisible mid-scream. A boy grew vines from his arms and wrapped them around a disco ball, pulling it down like a piñata.

Maya’s glow pulsed in sync with the music. Lex hovered slightly off the ground, wings twitching. And Zack was now halfway up the wall.

“I’m not sure this is safe,” Lex muttered.

“Who said anything about safe?” said Dr. Gene.


Chapter 3: The Choice

As the party intensified, the DJ—an android named CRSPR-X—announced the final hour of the night.

Dr. Gene gathered the guests in a circle under a spinning DNA chandelier.

“You have danced. You have evolved. Now you must choose,” they said. “At sunrise, your transformation will reverse—unless you drink the serum of permanence.”

A glowing table appeared, holding vials of neon green liquid. Each bore a DNA symbol.

“Keep your enhancements. Join a new evolution. Or walk back into the ordinary world.”

The room fell silent.

Zack looked down at his scaled hands. “I kinda like climbing walls…”

Maya shook her head. “This isn’t natural. We don’t know what we’re becoming.”

Lex looked at their shimmering wings. “What if we don’t fit in anywhere anymore?”

Then Maya spotted a hidden panel behind the stage. Curious, she opened it—and found a lab filled with journals, serum prototypes, and a flickering monitor showing failed experiments. Some names on the list had been crossed out.

“Guys… this isn’t a party. It’s a test,” she whispered. “We’re lab rats.”

Dr. Gene appeared behind her. “No, Maya. You’re pioneers.”

Zack, Maya, and Lex stood before the vials.

“I’m out,” Maya said. “I’ll stay human.”

Lex hesitated, then shook their head. “I need to figure out who I really am—on my own.”

Zack sighed. “Guess I’ll just climb walls the old-fashioned way.”

They stepped away.

With sunrise approaching, the party dissolved into light. The guests returned to normal, memories of the night fading like fog.

But Lex kept one feather, hidden in their coat.

And every Halloween after, the invitation returned—waiting for the next brave soul ready to discover who they really were inside.

THE END
