Chapter 1: The Hidden Garden

Tucked away behind a velvet hill and a curtain of whispering willows, there was a secret place that very few had ever seen. It was called The Heart-Shaped Garden, and from above, its emerald borders curled into the perfect shape of love—a great big heart.

No one knew who planted it, but the garden had been there for generations. Tall trees grew along its edges, their trunks curved and leaning slightly inward, as if embracing the space in the center. These weren’t ordinary trees—they were Fruit Trees of Wonder, and each one grew a different fruit with magical flavors.

One tree bore glowing golden apples that tasted like honey and sunshine. Another tree grew purple pears that giggled softly when picked. The lemon tree's fruit sparkled with a silvery shimmer and made you sing when you bit into one.


Chapter 2: The Guardian of the Garden

In the middle of the garden stood an old wooden bench, half-covered in ivy, facing a small sparkling pond. Next to the bench lived an old gardener named Mira. She wasn’t very tall, but she had bright eyes that twinkled like stars. Mira had been the Guardian of the Garden for as long as anyone could remember.

Each morning, she sang to the trees and watered their roots with dew collected from morning mist. The garden listened to her songs, and in return, it bloomed brighter each day.

Children from the nearby village had heard stories about the garden, but only those who came with kindness in their hearts could find it. If your intentions were selfish or greedy, the garden would vanish from sight, and you’d find only a patch of dry grass.


Chapter 3: A Gift for the World

One sunny afternoon, a little girl named Lila wandered through the woods with a basket of wildflowers. She had no idea she was near the heart-shaped garden. She just liked to hum while she picked flowers. As she walked through the trees, the air began to shimmer, and suddenly, she stepped into a clearing—right into the Heart-Shaped Garden.

Mira welcomed her with a smile. “You have the heart of a gardener,” she said.

Lila spent the whole afternoon helping Mira pick fruit, sweep leaves, and laugh with the pears. Before she left, Mira gave her a seed from the golden apple tree.

“Plant this where love is needed most,” Mira whispered.

Years later, gardens shaped like hearts began popping up in villages all over the land. Each one full of magical trees, laughter, and love—gifts from the very first garden, now shared with the world.
