Maze Runner

Victor Maze: Runner of the Stories

Chapter 1: Born into the Maze

Victor Maze woke up in the Box—a cold, steel coffin rising into light.

He couldn’t remember his name at first, only fragments: voices, falling skies, and pages turning in the dark. When the Box doors opened, he found himself in the Glade—an enormous field walled in by stone giants, pulsing with the hum of something ancient and intelligent.

They called it the Maze.

Others had been there before him. Runners. Builders. Cooks. Fighters. No one knew why they were there, only that they must solve the Maze to survive. But Victor—Victor felt something deeper. Like the Maze wasn’t just a trap. It was alive. Telling a story. His story.

He saw shapes in the vines. Letters in the wall rotations. Patterns in the Grievers’ movements. Every dead end whispered a sentence. Every twist, a question.

“The Maze is not just where I am,” he told himself. “It’s what I am in.”


Chapter 2: Reading the Maze

Victor became a Runner within days. He memorized the shifting paths and carried a notebook sewn from scraps of others’ maps. But instead of drawing exits, he began translating them like sentences.

He realized something: the Maze was a language. A living narrative etched in stone, memory, and loss.

Each section of the Maze told a different chapter:

  • Section 3 replayed his nightmares.
  • Section 7 echoed voices of people he couldn't remember.
  • Section 11 reset every day—as if it were a rewrite.

One night, trapped outside, Victor made it to the Maze’s edge. There, carved in smooth, hidden stone, he found the line:

"To reach the end, you must first read the beginning."

The Maze wasn’t meant to be escaped.

It was meant to be understood.


Chapter 3: Victor Writes the Ending

Victor stopped running to escape and started running to finish the story. He mapped the Maze’s rhythms like a book’s chapters, its loops like character arcs, and the hidden walls like plot twists waiting to unfold.

In the heart of the Maze, past the Griever dens and memory-holes, he found a door. Not locked—just waiting.
Inside: a chamber filled with screens, files, and one final manuscript.

He was ending the first volume of a larger saga.

The desert beyond didn’t scare him.
